Comprehensive Rental Property Management

Preparing the Property for Rent

  • visiting and assessing the property,
  • reviewing its current condition,
  • recommending minor improvements or additional furnishings,
  • preparing the property for viewings,
  • taking professional listing photographs,
  • creating an attractive rental description,
  • documenting the property's equipment and condition.

Starting the Tenancy

  • responding to enquiries from prospective tenants,
  • communicating with applicants,
  • organising property viewings,
  • carrying out an initial tenant screening,
  • agreeing the rental terms with the owner,
  • preparing a draft tenancy agreement,
  • preparing the property handover report,
  • recording utility meter readings,
  • handing over the property and keys to the tenant.

Ongoing Rental Management

  • maintaining regular communication with the tenant,
  • monitoring rental payments,
  • monitoring administrative charges,
  • monitoring utility payments,
  • preparing monthly summaries of income and expenses,
  • transferring the amounts due to the owner,
  • informing the owner about payment delays,
  • carrying out periodic property inspections,
  • maintaining the necessary rental documentation.

Maintenance and Repair Coordination

  • receiving maintenance requests by phone, text message or email,
  • assessing the nature and urgency of each issue,
  • arranging minor repairs,
  • contacting suitable contractors,
  • coordinating more complex work,
  • supervising plumbing, drainage, electrical and gas-related repairs,
  • informing the owner about estimated costs,
  • documenting completed work.

End of the Tenancy

  • arranging the property check-out,
  • collecting the keys,
  • inspecting the condition of the property,
  • comparing its condition with the original handover report,
  • recording final meter readings,
  • identifying any potential damage,
  • preparing a proposed deposit settlement,
  • coordinating work required before the next tenancy,
  • preparing the property for re-listing.

The exact scope of the service is defined in the management agreement. The owner retains control over major expenses and key decisions.

The Tenant Reports the Problem. We Coordinate the Next Steps.

Maintenance requests can be submitted by phone, text message or email.

Once a problem is reported, we assess its nature and urgency and determine the appropriate course of action.

Minor repairs up to an agreed spending limit may be arranged without requesting the owner's approval each time.

For larger expenses, we inform the owner about the proposed work and estimated cost. Repairs exceeding the agreed limit require the owner's approval.

The cost of materials, repairs and external contractors is covered by the property owner.

A Clearly Defined Scope of Responsibility

The standard rental management service does not include:

  • representing the owner before courts,
  • conducting administrative proceedings,
  • preparing the owner's tax returns,
  • financing repairs or external services,
  • carrying out major renovations,
  • guaranteeing that a tenant will be found within a specific period,
  • accepting responsibility for vacant periods,
  • guaranteeing that tenants will always pay on time,
  • accepting responsibility for the tenant's conduct,
  • accepting responsibility for damage caused by the tenant.

When a problem occurs, we take the actions defined in the management agreement, document the situation and provide the owner with the information required to make further decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does rental property management cost?

Can you take over an existing tenancy?

Yes. We can take over the management of an already occupied property after reviewing the tenancy agreement, existing documentation and previous arrangements.

Do you organise property viewings?

Do you screen prospective tenants?

Do you monitor rental payments?
